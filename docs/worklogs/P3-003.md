# P3-003 Worklog: Meetings and knowledge transfer

## Status

Complete.

## Goal

Make the Lantern Square meeting reflect real information asymmetry: agents arrive with different knowledge, share named signals through explicit events, revise individual beliefs, explain their disagreements, and leave behind a durable memo and next-mission proposal.

## Intended files

- `apps/orchestrator/src/expedition-runtime.ts`: deterministic multi-agent arrival coordination and meeting lifecycle driver.
- `packages/simulation/src/commands.ts`: meeting availability, participant, place-capability, and duplicate validation.
- `packages/simulation/src/reducer.ts`: strict requested/started/shared/memo/ended lifecycle invariants.
- `apps/web/src/world-shell/MeetingWorkspace.tsx`: Lantern Square knowledge, sharing, disagreement, belief, and memo scene.
- `apps/web/src/world-shell/WorldShell.tsx`: meeting command, event synchronization, skip-arrival control, and workspace routing.
- `apps/web/src/world-shell/WorldStageHost.tsx`: real Convene at Lantern Square action and busy-state guidance.
- `tests/e2e/meeting-workspace.spec.ts`: required evidence-to-meeting journey, Archive memo handoff, and visual regression.

## Boundaries

- A meeting begins only from a validated `meeting.request` command. The orchestrator, not the UI, owns participant travel, sharing, belief changes, memo creation, and completion.
- Agents can share only signals for which they already hold a knowledge edge. Recipients gain separate `agent.knowledge.acquired` events with `kind: shared`, the sender, and meeting ID.
- Meeting travel uses ordinary generated missions and ordinary route events. Skipping arrivals appends every remaining progress, arrival, and mission-completion event before the exchange.
- The UI reconstructs each participant's pre-meeting signal set from immutable spawn and knowledge events; it does not infer prior knowledge from the final projection.
- Skipping the discussion is presentation-only. The full share, knowledge, belief, memo, and end sequence is already in the event log and remains replayable.
- Disagreement labels describe distinct layers: evidence held, model/independence assumptions, and prior probability differences.

## Acceptance criteria

- Mira, Orin, and Kestrel can be convened at Lantern Square after their active and queued missions finish.
- Before the meeting, the scene shows Mira holding the weather signal, Orin holding the historical signal, and Kestrel holding neither.
- Meeting share events identify the sender, signal, recipients, and meeting; separate knowledge events update every recipient.
- Generated arrival missions can run normally or be skipped without omitting route or arrival events.
- The scene explains evidence, model, and prior disagreement in text and shows per-agent YES belief movement.
- A concise memo records agreements, disagreements, and a Professor correlation-check mission proposal.
- The memo becomes searchable under the Archive Quarter's Memo shelf.
- The scene and skip controls are keyboard-operable and preserve the global Escape return behavior.

## Results

- Added deterministic multi-agent meeting scheduling. A single request appends `meeting.requested`, creates ordinary `meet_agent` coordination missions for participants away from the square, starts all available routes together, and waits until every participant has actually arrived.
- Specialized natural and skipped completion of coordination missions so neither path invokes a fake source turn. The final arrival atomically starts the meeting and preserves contiguous event ordering.
- Added a scripted evidence exchange derived exclusively from participant knowledge at meeting start. Each non-universal signal produces one `meeting.signal_shared` event and one explicit shared-knowledge event per recipient.
- Added post-share agent belief updates for newly learned signals, using the same bounded directional impact logic as fixture investigation turns while retaining distinct previous/new probability distributions.
- Produced evidence, model, and prior disagreement labels from pre-meeting knowledge sets, available signal count, and participant beliefs. The memo turns those labels into plain-language explanations and proposes a bounded Professor independence check for Kestrel.
- Hardened the reducer so a meeting must match its request, shares stay within active participants, duplicate/late memos are rejected, and a memo is required before ending.
- Built a Lantern Square scene with arrival proof, three participant knowledge cards, explicit before/after columns, shared signal cards with sender and recipients, per-agent belief arrows, disagreement definitions, and a conventional memo panel.
- Wired the world footer's existing visual affordance to a real command, disabled it while agents are busy, synchronized immutable meeting events into the scene, and added an explicit skip-arrivals workflow.
- Added a browser journey that discovers Mira and Orin's evidence, proves asymmetric pre-meeting knowledge, skips arrivals with preserved events, verifies all knowledge transfers and disagreement labels, skips presentation to the memo, and finds that memo in Archive Quarter.
- Added and manually reviewed the 1440 × 900 Lantern Square baseline. Participant knowledge, shared evidence, disagreement explanations, belief movement, event proof, and surrounding world rails remain legible.
