# P1-003 Worklog: Agent dock and sprites

## Status

Complete.

## Goal

Make Mira, Orin, and Kestrel immediately distinguishable as persistent characters and synchronize accessible React selection with rendered Phaser state in both directions.

## Intended files

- `packages/game-scene`: bounded sprite-state vocabulary, generated pixel frames, animations, selection acknowledgement, and reduced-motion control.
- `apps/web/src/world-shell`: richer fixture-derived agent presentation, role badges, follow controls, and scene synchronization.
- `tests/e2e/world-shell.spec.ts`: dock detail, React-to-canvas, canvas-to-React, follow, and reduced-motion coverage.

## Boundaries

- Sprite state reflects the authoritative agent public state; this task does not invent travel or mission events.
- Procedural placeholder frames follow the approved palette and character silhouettes. Commissioned bitmap art can replace textures without changing animation or bridge contracts.
- Follow and selection are view-state commands only.
- Mission dispatch, travel interpolation, and event-driven state transitions begin in P2.

## Acceptance criteria

- All three agents have distinct portraits, role badges, silhouettes, and accessories.
- Each sprite owns idle, walk, read/work, and share animation definitions at the approved low frame rate.
- The dock exposes status, location, active mission or explicit idle state, and forecast.
- Selecting through the dock or canvas updates both layers; a dock action follows each agent.
- The accessible agent list retains logical keyboard behavior and visible focus.
- Reduced motion visibly freezes sprite animation without hiding agent state.

## Results

- Added a bounded `idle | walk | work | share` sprite vocabulary with explicit mapping from every authoritative public agent state. Three agents × four states produce 36 collision-free procedural texture frames and low-rate Phaser animations (4–8 FPS).
- Generated distinct 12 × 16 base textures for each character and rendered them as crisp world sprites. Mira has a cyan field coat and radio, Orin has a violet coat, square glasses, and satchel, and Kestrel has a green coat, angular coral scarf, and notebook treatment.
- Replaced static camera markers with interactive Phaser sprites, per-agent selection rings, renderer acknowledgements, state-change commands, and follow targets.
- Added bidirectional selection: dock/keyboard selection sends `agent.select` and waits for rendered acknowledgement; canvas sprite activation emits `agent.selected` back to React.
- Added scene-level motion acknowledgement. Reduced-motion changes pause/resume all agent animations as well as ambient tweens, including preference changes after the lazy scene has mounted.
- Expanded each dock card with a distinct role badge, public state, full location, explicit mission state, forecast, knowledge badge, shortcut, and selected-agent Follow action. The command tray remains synchronized with selection.
- Hardened lazy initialization so the latest reduced-motion, agent, place, and follow view state is replayed when `scene.ready` arrives, avoiding lost commands during StrictMode startup.
- Reviewed updated 1440 × 900 and 1280 × 800 captures. All three agents remain recognizable in both the dock and world without displacing the dominant stage.

## Remaining risks

- The sprite frames are intentionally procedural vertical-slice art. They prove silhouette, state, input, and animation contracts, but a future commissioned sprite atlas should replace them without changing those contracts.
- Fixture agents are idle and have no active missions. Non-idle animation selection is exhaustively unit-tested; P2 will provide event-driven travel and work demonstrations in the product flow.
